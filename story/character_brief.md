# Hack The Planet — Character Brief

Lead-writer artifact. The Goal / Motivation / Conflict frame for the four speaking characters, plus voice notes pulled from existing dialogue and a per-stage arc position so any new line lands in the right register.

This is a **brief**, not a bible. Edit it as the story moves. The compiled `dialogue_brief.md` is the source of truth for what's actually in the files.

---

## Story frame

**Genre:** Cyberpunk. **Tonal touchstones:** *Hackers* (1995) — found-family of misfits, neon vibe, hacking-as-art swagger. *The Matrix* — the wire is a real second world; choosing your side is the plot.

**Premise:** The Gibson grid runs on a mesh of independent operators. One of them — Splice — has resurfaced after a year quiet, and is hunting a chain of power-ups that will hand him root access. If he gets it, the era of distributed hackers ends. The runner (player) is recruited from outside that world and pulled into the fight.

**Political axis:** Hackers-for-the-people (DialTone, Nyx) vs. centralizer-with-a-grudge (Splice). The runner picks a side by playing.

**Themes the dialogue should land:**
- **Belonging.** The runner wants in. Each NPC offers a different version of "in."
- **Found-family vs. burn-it-down.** The crew has each other; Splice is alone and tells himself that's the strong move.
- **Subtext over text.** Affection and fear are deflected, named only by accident.
- **Choice as identity.** The Splice offer is real. So is the bail-out. The runner becomes whoever they choose.

## DialTone

**Role:** The recruiter. The voice in your ear. Operates the channel.

### GMC

**Goal**
- *External:* Stop Splice from getting root access. Keep the Gibson distributed.
- *Internal:* Validate that he can pick people. The runner is the proof — or the disproof.

**Motivation**
- Believes hackers exist as a check on centralization. When one of them turns into a centralizer, that's the apocalypse the rest of them are sworn against.
- Has been "casting" — patiently watching forums for someone with the right asking-pattern. The runner is who he found. The whole rescue setup was the audition. He's only half-aware of how manipulative that read was.
- Personal stake with Splice: they've been duelling sub-rosa for a year. He's claimed publicly to be unbothered ("statistically immortal"). He isn't.

**Conflict**
- *External:* His node is flagged — he can't go in himself. Has to operate by proxy. Reliant on the runner.
- *Internal:* He has to be wrong about how serious this is *and* keep the crew functional. Started L1 as a prank because he didn't believe Splice was actually back. Now he has to carry the weight without admitting the misread.
- *Internal:* The "casting" reveal shifts the runner's relationship with him. He has to manage that gracefully or lose the runner to the sane choice.

### Voice

- **Performative.** Theatrical. Always selling. Bravado used as armor.
- **Compresses bad news.** "He's been quiet for a year. Now he's not. Don't love it." Three short clauses; the worry is in the rhythm.
- **Uses runner-speak unironically.** "Easiest hop on the grid." "Get on the board."
- **Names himself in the bit.** "I'm statistically immortal at this point." Half-joke, half-prayer.
- **When wrong, deflects to plan.** Doesn't apologize. Pivots to next move.

**Sample lines (canon):**
- "Listen — my friend Nyx is stuck between sectors. Need you to break her loose."
- "Take this — walkie's on my channel. I'll be in your ear the whole run."
- "Never gets old."
- "I wanted to see her face."
- "Worth every bit."
- "Alright — got the next ping queued. Softball, this time. Real one."

### Arc position by stage

| Stage | Where he is |
|-------|-------------|
| Pre-L1 | Selling. Confident. The whole thing is a bit. |
| Post-L1 | Caught in his own prank. Doubles down on bravado but reveals more than he meant ("the messenger thread"). Gives the runner a milk-run as apology disguised as commerce. |
| Post-L2 | **Punctured.** Splice is real and he had to admit it. Frames a legitimate plan: split up, find the thing first. Compliment the runner by giving them a peer's slice of the work. |
| Post-L3 | The chase is on. Splice's offer was a test — DialTone gets to admit he wasn't sure the runner would say no. |
| Post-L4 | Confession. The whole thing was an audition. He's done lying about it; the runner earned the truth. |

## Nyx

**Role:** The peer. The one who measures. The crush.

### GMC

**Goal**
- *External:* Stop Splice. Don't let him win — and don't let her past with him define this.
- *Internal:* Stay armored. Don't be the one who does saving — and don't be the one who needs it.

**Motivation**
- She used to run with Splice. ("Run is putting it lightly," in DialTone's words.) Ex. She regrets it. He represents a wrong call she can't take back.
- Hackers-for-the-people aligns with who she's chosen to be *since*. His resurfacing is the past coming for her.
- The runner pulls a string she hasn't felt in a while — peer, possibly more. Doesn't admit it. Won't.

**Conflict**
- *External:* She had to save the runner in L2. Contradicts her self-image as the operator who doesn't need help. She plays it casual.
- *Internal:* Wants the runner to walk away (sane move) AND wants them to stay (selfish). She offers the exit ramp because the wanting them to stay is the part that scares her.
- *Internal:* Splice knows her too well. If he gets root, *she* failed to read him a year ago. Her stake in the fight is half-atonement.

### Voice

- **Deadpan.** Numbers, corrections, single-word echoes. "Twelve."
- **Score-keeping.** "He said that to me. Thirteen times." She remembers everything. The remembering IS the affection.
- **Affection comes out as concern dressed as dismissal.** "Don't read into it." "Mm. We'll see."
- **Off-channel.** When she's serious, she comes find you alone. The fact she came IS the confession.
- **Doesn't compliment.** Closest she gets is *not insulting* — "Decent." She'd rather you misread her as cold.

**Sample lines (canon):**
- "How many times have you pulled that same stunt, really? Does it not get old for you?"
- "He's stalling."
- "Hope it was worth it."
- "I pulled your trace. He'll round it down. I'm not going to."
- "I pull everyone's trace. Don't read into it."
- "Mm. We'll see."

### Arc position by stage

| Stage | Where she is |
|-------|-------------|
| Pre-L1 (her own level) | Caught off-guard, mildly mortified. "He's a child." Annoyed at DialTone, not the runner. |
| Post-L1 | Off-channel for the first time. Skeptical, score-keeping, daring the runner not to be DialTone's pet project. The flirt is in "I checked." |
| Post-L2 | **Equal-footing now.** She rescued the runner — plays it casual ("anyone would have needed pulling out of that"). The flirt has worry in it. Confides that logging out is the sane move; she'd understand. **Doesn't say "don't go." Says "you don't have to." That's the line.** |
| Post-L3 | The bail-out wasn't taken. She drops one layer of armor. Maybe the first compliment that isn't a deflection. |
| Post-L4 | The end. The perimeter party. She's *won*. Splice is boxed; she's dancing where he can see. |

## Splice

**Role:** The villain. The mirror. The path not taken.

### GMC

**Goal**
- *External:* Root access to the Gibson. Step one of a chain of power-ups; each unlocks the next. Final state: total control of the grid. Hackers-as-class become obsolete.
- *Internal:* Win. Be the one who saw it clearly when the rest of them were playing pretend.

**Motivation**
- Was bullied. Felt the world was rigged. Now plays the game ruthlessly because "this is how the game is played." Got good at it.
- VC / silicon-valley climber mindset. *More is more. We can take over.* The growth is the point. The original wrongs are background — he stopped tracking them years ago, but the hunger they made is still the engine.
- Believes everyone else is sheeple — credulous, performative, gameable. Sees DialTone's "hackers for the people" stance as a marketing pose. He may be partially right; that's why it stings.

**Conflict**
- *External:* DialTone and Nyx know him. They're the only crew with both the skill and the motive to stop him.
- *External:* Has to convert the runner — or eliminate them — before the runner is too embedded with the others.
- *Internal:* The calm/amused/jaded affect is real until refused. After refusal, the original wound shows: anger, desperation, the bullied kid screaming through the operator. **By L4 he cracks.**
- *Internal:* Nyx represents the only version of his life where he didn't become this. Avoids her until forced.

### Voice

- **Calm. Amused. Jaded.** Speaks as if he's already seen the bigger picture and is patient with you for not seeing it yet.
- **Conspiracy-flavored.** Throws lines like "the Gibson's about to belong to whoever's smart enough to take it." Marketing-pitch cadence with a knife under it.
- **Compliments by separating you from the crew.** "You're built different. The crew you're running with? They don't see it." The flattery is the wedge.
- **Repeats his own words for emphasis when excited.** "I knew. I KNEW."
- **Loses the calm when refused.** "wow. Wow okay." Dropping consonants. Bullied kid breaking through.
- **By L4, anger.** Threats stop being veiled.

**Sample lines (canon):**
- "Interesting. Do I detect a new trace on the wire?"
- "You're built different. The crew you're running with? They don't see it."
- "Just say the word, runner. Just say it."
- "...wow. Wow okay. Your loss, runner. Truly."
- "I told you not to move."

### Arc position by stage

| Stage | Where he is |
|-------|-------------|
| L2 walkies | First contact. Calm, amused. Lets the crew know he sees them. |
| L3 (offer) | Courtship. Pitches the runner privately. Patient. Confident. |
| Post-refusal (L3) | Crack one — "wow. Wow okay." Drops the act for a beat, then exits. |
| L4 | **Desperation.** If denied through L4, he gets visibly angry. The original-wound voice surfaces. |
| Post-L4 (loyal route) | Quarantined. Boxed but not gone. The perimeter holds because the crew holds. |

## The Runner (Player)

**Role:** The reader-stand-in. New to the grid. Not naive.

### GMC

**Goal**
- *External:* Survive each level. Pick a side that fits.
- *Internal:* **Belonging.** To be *in*. The forums weren't enough.

**Motivation**
- Was approached because they asked the right questions on neon-archive / 0xCirca. Curious, smart, somewhat lonely. Saw the message. Came.
- Has a crush on Nyx. Won't say it; wouldn't even know how. Reads her score-keeping as the closest thing to recognition they've ever gotten.
- Wants to know what Splice sees. Not because they want to defect — because they want to know if there's something the others are missing.

**Conflict**
- *External:* Three voices pull on them — DialTone's plan, Nyx's exit ramp, Splice's offer. None of them lie outright. All of them frame.
- *Internal:* "Belonging" can be earned (DialTone's audition) or chosen (Nyx's quiet "you don't have to"). Splice offers a third version: you don't need them. Each version is true partway.

### Voice

- **Wordless.** No spoken dialogue. The runner expresses agency through menu choices.
- **Choice text reads as the runner's sub-vocal stance.** Curious. Ready to push back. Not eager to please.
- **Range:**
  - With **DialTone** — *testing his frame.* "Are you who messaged me?" "Why can't you go yourself?" Neither hostile nor compliant.
  - With **Nyx** — *warmer, but still measured.* "Why off-channel?" "What do you want?" The choices that read as flirt should be subtle ("DialTone..").
  - With **Splice** — *provocative.* "Tell me more." Curiosity, not commitment. The interesting branch is asking the question, not taking the offer.
- **Choices the player should always have available:** at least one walk-away option per branch. The runner is never trapped into a beat.

### Arc position by stage

| Stage | Where the runner is |
|-------|---------------------|
| Pre-L1 | New. Curious. Says yes because someone finally asked. |
| Post-L1 | Realizes it was a prank. Has the option to be miffed, amused, or coolly noting it. |
| Post-L2 | Has been hurt. Splice is real. The exit ramp is on the table. |
| L3 | Faces the offer directly. The choice is the choice. |
| Post-L3 | If loyal: in for the rest. The crew now treats them as such. |
| L4 | Endgame. The runner is the operator who pushes Splice into quarantine. |

## Cross-character notes

### The Nyx ↔ Splice past

- Reveal: **Splice is Nyx's ex.** ("Used to run with him." DialTone: "Run is putting it lightly.")
- Surfaces in: post-L2 DialTone scene with Nyx present. If the player pulls the thread, the reveal lands as Nyx's line "She regrets it" — or Nyx leaves the reveal to DialTone and just exits the scene.
- Doesn't surface again unless the player asks. Nyx will not volunteer it twice.

### The messenger thread

- Counter on `dialtone_messenger_thread` (set in `dial_tone.dialogue`). Increments at each post-Lx callback if the player keeps pulling it.
- Pre-L1: 0 (default)
- After intro_messenger: 1
- After post_1_messenger: 2
- After post_2_messenger: 3 — DialTone's recruitment story expands. Around here he should land: *"honestly, you can clearly run with the best of them. If you want in, you're in."*
- After post_3_messenger: 4
- After post_4_messenger: 5 — full reveal: it was an audition. The runner's IN.

The thread is structurally optional but emotionally load-bearing. A player who never pulls it gets a working game; a player who does gets the second story underneath.

### The "found-family" register

- DialTone and Nyx bicker constantly. They are not romantic. They are old-married-couple co-workers.
- Their banter does the heavy lifting on world-building. When one corrects the other ("Twelve."), the audience reads how long they've been doing this.
- The runner is never the third wheel — they're the audience for the bit that the bit is for. By post-L2 they're invited into it, not just watching it.
